My Flights Web App

The Challenge: Travelers often struggle to maintain a centralized, visual history of their flight data across different airlines.

Solution: I developed a full-stack application using the MERN stack (MongoDB, Express, React, Node.js). The app allows users to log flights, visualize their travel routes on an interactive map, and calculate total distance traveled.

Key Features:

  • RESTful API integration for real-time flight status.
  • Secure User Authentication using JWT.
  • Dynamic dashboard for personal travel statistics.

Competing at the WCA World Championship 2025: An Experience of a Lifetime

In 2025, I had the incredible opportunity to compete in the WCA World Championship, an event that brought together 2,000 competitors from 80 countries. Representing my country on such a global stage was beyond thrilling...

One of the proudest moments of my life was being chosen as a flag bearer during the opening ceremony. Carrying my country’s flag while walking among so many talented individuals I had admired growing up was deeply emotional—it was a culmination of years of practice, dedication, and dreams that suddenly felt real.

Beyond competing, I also volunteered at the event, helping organizers ensure the competition ran smoothly. Being part of the behind-the-scenes efforts gave me a new appreciation for the teamwork and planning required to run such a massive event.

The championship lasted six days, and on the last day of my trip, I decided to explore Seattle alone. I visited the Space Needle, wandered through the aquarium, and finally explored the Space Museum and Museum of Flight, places I had dreamed of visiting since I was a kid.

Living in a small campus town like Saginaw, life tends to move slowly. Trips like this remind me of how vast and colorful the world is. It gave me perspective, inspiration, and a deep sense of gratitude.

Competing at the WCA World Championship 2025: An Experience of a Lifetime

In 2025, I had the incredible opportunity to compete in the WCA World Championship, an event that brought together 2,000 competitors from 80 countries. Representing my country on such a global stage was beyond thrilling, and I participated in eight different events, each pushing me to test my skills, focus, and resilience in ways I never imagined.

One of the proudest moments of my life was being chosen as a flag bearer during the opening ceremony. Carrying my country’s flag while walking among so many talented individuals I had admired growing up was deeply emotional—it was a culmination of years of practice, dedication, and dreams that suddenly felt real.

Beyond competing, I also volunteered at the event, helping organizers ensure the competition ran smoothly. Being part of the behind-the-scenes efforts gave me a new appreciation for the teamwork and planning required to run such a massive event.

The championship lasted six days, and on the last day of my trip, I decided to explore Seattle alone. That solo adventure turned out to be just as memorable as the competition itself. I visited the Space Needle, wandered through the aquarium, rode the Loop scooters downtown, and finally explored the Space Museum and Museum of Flight. Walking the streets of a vibrant city alone gave me space for self-reflection and allowed me to appreciate the scale, diversity, and beauty of the world around me.

Living in a small campus town like Saginaw, life tends to move slowly. Trips like this remind me of how vast and colorful the world is, and they make me grateful for the quiet, steady life I have at home. Seeing the world in such a concentrated, vivid way gave me perspective, inspiration, and a deep sense of gratitude.

The WCA World Championship 2025 was more than just a competition—it was an emotional journey, a celebration of global talent, and a personal adventure.
